## XII. Recovery Convergence in Lorentzian Coordinates

The DISSOLUTION THEOREM specified three recovery regimes: jump revision (Regime 1), calibration convergence (Regime 2), and asymptotic approach to Ψ(O,i) (Regime 3). The GRI framework identified three analogous regimes in the learning context: rapid loss decrease following escape from a sharp minimum, slower convergence in the flat minimum neighborhood, and asymptotic approach to the global minimum. These are the same regimes in the same Lorentzian coordinates.

**Regime 1 (Jump Revision / Grokking):** Following the nucleation event (the first above-threshold demonstration satisfying D1 ∧ D2), the belief state undergoes rapid revision from the Berk-Nash stable point toward the event horizon and beyond. The rate:

> dσ_CE/dτ ∝ |x_max − x°| / prior_cycle_count

corresponds to the QMI framework's phase-1 convergence rate after C_α crosses 1: the Lorentz factor γ diverges, proper time slows, and the belief state undergoes maximum revision per coordinate-time unit. The jump-stabilization curve is the trajectory of a parameter state that has just exceeded the Schwarzschild radius — rapid initial motion (escape velocity exceeded), followed by deceleration as the trajectory reaches the region of lower curvature near the optimal state.

**Regime 2 (Calibration Convergence):** Following new Berk-Nash stabilization in G′'s core evaluative contexts, the residual schema activation persists in peripheral evaluative contexts — those structurally similar to the original proximate-threat configuration. Regime 2 is the gradual extension of the revised belief across the full context class, driven by the accumulation of confirming disconfirmation across novel evaluative environments. This is analogous to post-grokking generalization extending from the training distribution to new data distributions (Draxler et al., 2018, on essentially-no-barriers connectivity in the post-grokking loss landscape).

**Regime 3 (Asymptotic Approach):** Full convergence E(O,i,t) → Ψ(O,i) is approached asymptotically, with rate determined by the breadth of evaluative contexts providing confirming data, the completeness of overhead neutralization, and the absence of re-entrapment. In Lorentzian coordinates: the belief state approaches the global minimum (σ_CE = 0, σ_SE = 0, x_obs = x_max) along a geodesic that asymptotically approaches the minimum as the residual curvature from prior-cycle Berk-Nash stabilization dissipates.

Full convergence is not guaranteed in finite time — it requires the persistent absence of proximate-threat reactivation (which would add positive curvature to the now-flat manifold region near the optimal state) and the complete dissipation of the prior-cycle mass M_belief from the Schwarzschild metric. The connection to Seligman's (1975) learned helplessness generalization is exact: helplessness generalizes when uncontrollability is experienced across domains (across multiple evaluative contexts), and reversal requires controllable positive outcomes in specific domains (Regime 2's context-by-context calibration) before generalization to the full context class (Regime 3).
